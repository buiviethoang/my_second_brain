## Listening
### take down
= to write something that another person has just said:
- He took down my address and phone number and said he'd call me.
### courier
**UK  /ˈkʊr.i.ər/ US  /ˈkʊr.i.ɚ/**
= a person or company that takes messages, letters, or parcels from one person or place to another:
- I want to have this package delivered by motorcycle courier.
### set about sth
= to start to do or deal with something:
- I have no idea how to set about changing a tyre on a car.
- I tried to apologize, but I think I set about it the wrong way.
### refurbishment
**UK  /ˌriːˈfɜː.bɪʃ.mənt/ US  /ˌriːˈfɝː.bɪʃ.mənt/**
= work such as painting, repairing, and cleaning that is done to make a building look new again:
- The museum has undergone a £15 million refurbishment.
### legroom
= the amount of space available for your legs when you are sitting behind another seat:
- a car with plenty of legroom
### foyer
**UK  /ˈfɔɪ.eɪ/ US  /ˈfɔɪ.ɚ/**
= a large open area just inside the entrance of a public building such as a theatre or a hotel, where people can wait and meet each other:
### make a fuss of someone
= to become upset or excited:
- She was never one to fuss about insignificant things.
- Some people like to be fussed over (= receive a lot of attention).
### straightforward
**UK  /ˌstreɪtˈfɔː.wəd/ US  /ˌstreɪtˈfɔːr.wɚd/**
= easy to understand or simple:
- Just follow the signs to Bradford - it's very straightforward.
### amendment
**UK  /əˈmend.mənt/ US  /əˈmend.mənt/**
= a change or changes made to the words of a text:
- He insisted that the book did not need amendment.
- I've made a few last-minute amendments to the article.
### dry (something) out
= to make something dry, or to become dry:
- If you don't keep food covered, it dries out.
### run-down
= Run-down buildings or areas are in very bad condition:
- a run-down building/cemetery
### high-rise
=  a tall modern building with many floors:
- She lives in a high-rise overlooking the river.
### off-putting
= slightly unpleasant or worrying so that you do not want to get involved in any way:
- He's slightly aggressive, which a lot of people find off-putting when they first meet him.
- What I found off-putting was the amount of work that you were expected to do.
## Reading 
### seabed
= the solid surface of the earth that lies under the ocean:
- The ship has been lying on the seabed for more than 50 years.
### overladen
*a*
**UK/ˌəʊ.vəˈleɪ.dən/ US/ˌoʊ.vɚˈleɪ.dən/**
= carrying, holding, or containing too much of something:
- overladen with A waitress was serving food from a table overladen with meats, vegetables, salads and sweets.
- A few people protested that the boat was over-laden, but the captain ignored their concerns.
### mishandle
= to deal with something without the necessary care or skill:
- The police were accused of mishandling the investigation.
### undisputed
= If something is undisputed, everyone agrees about it:
- an undisputed fact
- the undisputed champion/winner
### starboard
= the right side of a ship or aircraft as you are facing forward
### hull

### port 
= the left side of a ship or aircraft as you are facing forward
### degradation
**UK  /ˌdeɡ.rəˈdeɪ.ʃən/ US  /ˌdeɡ.rəˈdeɪ.ʃən/**

- environmental degradation
### obstruction
**UK  /əbˈstrʌk.ʃən/ US  /əbˈstrʌk.ʃən/**
= something that blocks a road, passage, entrance, etc. so that nothing can go along it, or the act of blocking something in this way:
- There's some kind of obstruction on the railway tracks.
### protrude
*v*
**UK  /prəˈtruːd/ US  /prəˈtruːd/**
= to stick out from or through something:
- A rotting branch protruded from the swamp like a ghostly arm.
- protruding ears/teeth
### intermittently
= in a way that does not happen regularly or continuously; in a way that stops and starts repeatedly or with periods in between:
- We've discussed this problem intermittently, but so far we've failed to come up with a solution.
- Lightning flashed intermittently in an otherwise clear sky.
### obscurity
**UK  /əbˈskjʊə.rə.ti/ US  /əbˈskjʊr.ə.t̬i/**
= the state of not being known to many people:
- He was briefly famous in his twenties but then sank into obscurity.
- He rose from relative obscurity to worldwide recognition.
### in conjunction with
= together.
- "herbal medicine was used **in conjunction with** acupuncture and massage"
### stray
= to travel along a route that was not originally intended, or to move outside a limited area:
- A herd of cattle had strayed into the road.
- They got lost when they strayed too far from the path.
### climax
= the most important or exciting point in a story or situation, especially when this happens near the end:
- The climax of the air show was a daring flying display.
- The election campaign reaches its climax next week.
### trove
= a large amount of something good, useful, or valuable, or a place where this can be found:
- The college holds a vast trove of documents relating to the politician.
- One of the largest troves of consumer data is kept by a Colorado market research firm.
### house
= to give a person or animal a place to live, or to provide space for something:
- It will be difficult to house all the refugees.
- The museum houses the biggest collection of antique toys in Europe.
### artefact
= an object that has been made by a person, such as a tool or a decoration, especially one that is of historical interest:
- The museum's collection includes artefacts dating back to prehistoric times.
- The house displays a number of paintings of the poet, as well as his typewriter and other personal artefacts.
### salvage
= to save goods from being damaged or destroyed, especially from a ship that has sunk or been damaged, or a building that has been damaged by fire or a flood:
- Workers are attempting to salvage timber in the aftermath of the hurricanes.
### go-ahead
= an occasion when permission is given for someone to start doing something or for an event or activity to happen:
- give the go-ahead The government has given the go-ahead for a multi-billion pound road-building project.
- get the go-ahead We're ready to start but we're still waiting to get the go-ahead from our head office.
